# NanoTrasen Internal Security Guidelines
NanoTrasen stations within the core systems operate under an abbreviated set of guidelines to ensure safety, regulatory compliance, and profitability for our research outpost teams. These guidelines are known as the Internal Security Guidelines, the ISG, or “Space Law” informally.

The ISG applies to all sapients in the vicinity of a NanoTrasen Research station, including hostile factions and other individuals or organizations operating within NanoTrasen orbital space.
The Security Department aboard a NanoTrasen installation enforces and abides by the ISG. The goal of the Security Department is to make the station a safe and productive place for everyone on board. Do your best to uphold this simple directive by mediating conflicts, assisting the crew, and using the minimal required force. Always first consider if an arrest is required to resolve a conflict. 
- The Security Department is required to both enforce the ISG and abide by it.
  - Command Crew are expected to abide by the ISG.
- On NanoTrasen stations, the ISG is enforced by Security and the Head of Security, and they operate independently of the normal command structure ONLY in matters related to enforcement of the ISG.
# Definitions
## Standard Operating Procedures
1. The **Commanding Officer** (CO) is defined as whoever is acting as the Captain of a NanoTrasen station, and thus has the final say on law enforcement of the station. This can only be overruled by higher officials such as **Central Command** or **SolGov** (the Trans-Solar United Government of Earth).
2. The **Executive Officer** (XO) is the nominated second-in-command behind the CO. The XO receives only the additional privileges, rank, and authority directly named in the document below. The XO is expected to assume the responsibilities of the CO should they be incapacitated, deemed incapable via a Vote of No Confidence, or be found absent without obvious leave.
3. Members of the Security Department are still a part of the crew, and they only hold authority over the crew in matters of The ISG. Security may not give other crew orders beyond matters relating to upholding the ISG.
4. A **Trusted Authority** is any role which Central Command places importance and trust in, defined as any role that is expected to be Mindshielded.
	   - A **Relevant Authority** is any Trusted Authority that is relevant to the situation at hand. In the matters of a Warrant, this is likely to be a Head of Department, the Warden, the Head of Security, or the CO or XO.
5. A **Senior Member** of a Department is defined as the most experienced or trusted member or members of that department. The Head of Department, Acting Head of Department, and anyone with a Senior PDA or Apparel automatically count as a Senior Member, and if none are present the Department should vote on a single representative Senior Member.
	- A **Junior Member** of a Department is defined as any of the Learner Roles, and anyone wearing a Novice Pin. They are ineligible to be an Acting Head or a Senior Member of a Department. The Learner Roles are as follows:
		- Security Cadet
		- Medical Intern
		- Supply Assistant
		- Research Assistant
		- Technical Assistant
	- Heads of Department and Acting Heads of Department should be the primary educators for Junior Members of a department, with Senior Members being secondary educators. The Captain may serve as a tertiary educator should relevant staff be unavailable.
6. A Security Guard cannot be punished for ignoring orders that go against the ISG. They have a Right and Duty to pursue an arrest against orders if that arrest complies with the ISG and they believe it to be necessary. A Superior Officer cannot order a Security Guard to ignore an arrest they have committed to unless that order complies with the ISG.
	  -  The CO should endeavour to protect Security Guards who exercise this Right and Duty.
7. Breaches of the ISG by Security Guards, Command, or the Head of Security in any capacity **cannot be ignored.** Security Guards, Command, or the Head of Security breaking the ISG in any capacity cannot be ignored.
	- Members of Security who refuse or fail to uphold the ISG can be charged with ***Obstruction of Justice*** or ***Dereliction of Authority***.

## Jurisdiction
The Security Department only has Jurisdiction to enforce the ISG **on board** NanoTrasen Research Stations, Trade Stations, and Shuttles, and **within 1 Mass Scanner Radius** of NanoTrasen Research Stations, Trade Stations, and Shuttles as measured from the Mass Scanner console on the Bridge.
The Security Department also has Jurisdiction on any wrecks, debris, or asteroids pulled in by a NanoTrasen Salvage Magnet.

Vessels that dock to a NanoTrasen station become part of NT Jurisdiction regardless of their origins, and NT security guards may pursue criminals aboard vessels under ‘hot pursuit’ doctrine. If a vessel departs after committing a crime, NT security may intervene up to the point where the belligerent vessel leaves NT orbital space. 

NanoTrasen Security Guards **do not** have Jurisdiction aboard the Central Command Management & Transit Terminal, informally referred to as **“CentComm”**. A NanoTrasen Station Security Guard may only pursue arrests up to the point of docking at CentComm, after which the automated security system of CentComm should be trusted to handle and track any convicts.
Security personnel are encouraged to **parole** any remaining inmates in their care aboard the Central Command Management & Transit Terminal, as long as they have not been deemed a danger to themselves or the crew by the **CMO** or by **Central Command Staff**.

Anyone aboard a Central Command station answers directly to Central Command's **Internal Compliance Taskforce** (ICT), not to be confused with the Epsilon Protocol.
In the event of a crime aboard a Central Command Management & Transit Terminal, the ICT has the authority to alter or terminate employee contracts and to modify or wipe DNA backups.

Crimes committed within the orbital space of a Central Command Station may also further be passed to a SolGov Provost for investigation.
### Non-NanoTrasen Individuals and Entities
Sometimes, Non-NanoTrasen individuals and entities enter NanoTrasen orbital space. All Non-NanoTrasen individuals and entities for the purposes of the ISG and Standard Operating Procedure are either classed as Non-Aligned or Enemies of the Corporation.
### Non-Aligned
Non-Aligned individuals and entities are classified as being outside of NanoTrasen influence or employment, but still deserving of rights under SolGov Charters.
These include but are not limited to:
- Paradox Clones
- Syndicate Refugees
- Visitors
- Derelict Cyborg Units
Non-Aligned individuals and entities can be afforded the same rights, privileges, and protections as a regular crew member under the ISG on the condition that they **submit to a search** and are **issued valid NanoTrasen Station Identification** which is to be displayed at all times while on board a NanoTrasen vessel.
### Enemies of the Corporation
Enemies of the Corporation (EoC) are people or entities who are from factions actively hostile to NanoTrasen and are thus not protected by SolGov Charters.
These include but are not limited to:
- Nuclear Operatives (working as a team or alone)
- Cybersun Assassin Operatives
- Space Dragons
- Wizards of the Wizard Federation
- Space Pirates of the Freebooters Union
- Spider Clan Mercenaries (aka “Ninjas”).
Enemies of the Corporation are not to be afforded protections under the ISG and should not be allowed to harm people or profit.
## Reasonable Suspicion
**Reasonable Suspicion** is defined as when circumstantial evidence points to a **high likelihood** that a crew member has or is about to commit a crime, **but insufficient evidence to prove** this to be the case. It is rooted in evidence and logical deduction, and is not based on grudges, hunches, or other guesswork.
Reasonable suspicion **may allow a search**, but not arrests or sentencing.

**Examples:**
Reasonable Suspicion:
- *"We should search Urist McHands, I heard him talking about having contraband."*

Not Reasonable Suspicion:
- *"We should search Urist McSalvager, their job tends to turn up contraband."*
## Probable Cause
**Probable Cause** is defined as behaviour or actions that **any reasonable person would be convinced** that a crime has or is taking place, supported by physical or anecdotal evidence.
Probable Cause is required to conduct an arrest and/or sentencing.

**Examples:**
Probable Cause:
- *"We should arrest Urist McHands, a pair of witnesses told me he broke into cargo."*

Not Probable Cause:
- *"We should arrest Urist McHands, I saw him talking in maints with a known syndicate agent."* (but might be Reasonable Suspicion.)
- *"We should arrest Urist McHands, Urist McVox, and Urist McPositronic for all having their access logs on a door that was breached."* (the crime cannot be pinned on one individual and the net is cast too wide.)
## Contraband
Certain items and objects present a **possible or definite danger** to the crew or station, and should be handled accordingly.
There are four categories of Contraband: Departmental Contraband, Light Contraband, Contraband Substances, and Enemy Contraband.

The Security Department and Command Members should **never use Enemy Contraband**, and should refrain from using any contraband outside Red Alert or greater.

Permits can be granted for any Non-Enemy Contraband and **a permit should be offered first** instead of confiscating Contraband, as long as said Contraband is not linked to a crime.

The Production and/or Supply of contraband without a valid Permit should be charged with **Black Marketeering**. Any methods of Production should be halted, and any Supplied contraband should be confiscated.

For more information, see the [dedicated Contraband page](/rules/internal-security-guidelines/contraband).
# Warrants & Permits
Warrants & Permits cover any Notarized paperwork that grant permissions not present under standard operations. Verbal agreements and Security records **cannot** function as Notarized paperwork.

For paperwork to be Notarized, it **must be stamped** by a Relevant Authority. Notarized paperwork should be copied and filed for safety, such as in the Relevant Authority's office or in the office of the Head of Personnel.
The CO and XO may stand in for any absent or unavailable Relevant Authorities, but should endeavour not to Notarize any paperwork that grants themselves extra permissions wherever possible.

Permits & Warrants must be narrow in their scope. They should clearly define **what** permissions they grant, **why** they grant them, **who** they grant them to, and **for how long**.

The Lawyer may, at any point, request to review any Permit or Warrant.
## Search Warrants
There are two forms of Search Warrant: **Personnel** Search Warrants and **Departmental** Search Warrants.

A Personnel Search Warrant must be Notarized by the Warden or Head of Security.

Departmental Search Warrants must be Notarized by the relevant Head of Department if present and available, or any Senior Member of said Department if they are not.

The Warden or Head of Security may still choose to stamp a relevant Department Search Warrant to affirm the authority being used to request its notarization by the relevant authority.

Departmental Search Warrants must specify whether or not they allow the search of personnel.
## Other Warrants
Warrants should also be written to document any accepted terms for Community Service or Parole.
These Warrants can be written after any such sentences have been started, and should seek to document and codify any arrangements already in place. A copy of a Parole Warrant should be handed to the Paroled inmate and their Parole Authority.

The Warden may write and Notarize an Arrest Warrant if an employee marked for Arrest is located behind access locked doors. An Arrest Warrant grants the Security Department temporary authority to enter a specific Department for the purpose of arresting an individual for concrete, provable crimes.
Arrest Warrants are legally binding for any Trusted Authority as long as they are valid and verifiable.

To authorize an Execution Trial, an Execution Warrant should be faxed to Central Command, notarized by either the Head of Security or CO. This Warrant **must** cover the fundamental arguments the Security Department is planning to use to justify Execution during the Trial.
In the event of an execution due to a **Breach of Permanent Confinement**, an Execution Warrant should still be faxed to Central Command. It should state that a Breach of Permanent Confinement was performed and that the right to Trial was thus waivered.
## Security Permits
Security Permits can allow members of the crew to produce, store, utilize, and/or supply Light Contraband.
Security Guards and the Lawyer can grant these Permits without Notarizing them, however Unnotarized Security Permits are liable to be revoked by the Warden, Head of Security, XO, or CO for any reason.

The Warden and Head of Security can Notarize Security Permits, and these cannot be revoked without proof of abuse.
## Other Permits
For a Permit for Departmental Equipment, the Head of the Department the equipment belongs to is the Relevant Authority.

In matters of Power, Atmosphere, and the Hull of the station, the Chief Engineer is the Relevant Authority.

A Permit for Contraband Substances is known as a Legal Prescription, and can be Notarized by the Chief Medical Officer, Acting Chief Medical Officer, or Senior Chemist.

Building Permits can be Notarized by the Head of Personnel or Chief Engineer.
# Search & Seizure
Sometimes its required for members of the Security Department to search a member of the NanoTrasen staff for evidence of a crime or contraband. Searches should only be completed with good reason to believe something will be found.

Any and all searches may be **declined on Green Alert**.
On Blue Alert or higher, a member of the Security Department may complete a search if they **have Reasonable Suspicion and a Warrant**. They may also perform a search without a Warrant if they **have established Probable Cause.**
On Red Alert, searches may be completed at the discretion of the Security Department without a Warrant. Red Alert also grants the Security Department the authority to perform random searches of any and all personnel.
## Personnel Searches
A Personnel Search is the seizure of the objects in a person's bag, hands, coat storage, belt, and pockets. This may be completed on the spot or in a secure location, such as the brig or a security checkpoint. The searched personnel can request to be searched in a secure location.

Storage of a private, dangerous, or vulnerable nature (such as a Secure Briefcase, Locked Crate, inside a Plushie, etc.) may **only** be performed in a secure and private location.
## Departmental Searches
Departmental searches are a sweep of an entire department on board a station for evidence and contraband. Departmental searches are **not**:
- Security being allowed in to detain someone inside that department. (That is an arrest)
- Security investigating an area for evidence. (That is Crime Scene Investigation)

Given the often decentralized nature of the Service Department, searches thereof should be confined to a room or rooms that are meaningfully connected.
The definitions of each room will change on a per-station basis, however some general examples include:
Catering (Botany, Bar, and Kitchen), Theatre (Stage, Backstage, Performers Rooms), Janitorial, Chapel, Library, Reporting Room, etc.

Departmental searches may only be performed under the direct watch of the Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator. They **require** a valid Department Search Warrant to perform.
A CO may only authorize a departmental search without a warrant if all available Relevant Authorities capable of notarizing the warrant are suspected in a relevant crime.

When executing a Departmental Search, the Security Department should aim to be as thorough as possible. They have the authority to check any locked storage, and to make use of a t-ray scanner.

Any Contraband without a valid permit found during a Departmental Search may be confiscated without issue, and offering a permit is not necessary.

Any evidence located during a Departmental Search should be handed over to the overseeing Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator.
# Arrest Procedure
The Arrest Procedure is what an **ideal arrest, in a vacuum**, should look like. Not all arrests can or will follow these guidelines due to the nature of the job, but sticking to them where you can should help **prevent friction with the crew**.
1. When spotting someone marked for arrest, you should **flag them down audibly** - either verbally with their name, or via a whistle whilst pointing at them. It should **be clear** that you wish for **them to cease movement**, and **who** you are flagging down.
	- Make every effort to flag them down before attempting any sort of restraining
	- If they do not respond to at least three hailing, you may utilize bolas or disablers.
2. **Inform the individual** that they are marked as wanted in relation to a crime, and **what crime or crimes** that is. Show any relevant warrants and ensure they understand what they are being detained for.
3. If they are compliant, cuff and transport the prisoner to the brig without causing any undue harm. If they resist arrest, use your non-lethal/less-than-lethal equipment to detain the crewmember.
	- Should the crewmember attempt to violently or lethally resist arrest, or otherwise render your non-lethal means ineffective, you may use any lethal means to bring them to a point where they are incapacitated and able to be restrained. Subduing someone **should never require killing** them unless absolutely necessary, and any **dead suspects should provoke investigation** for ***Neglect of Duty*** or use of excessive force from the arresting Guard. 
4. If lethal force was used, **medical aid must be provided**, located, or requested to stabilize the crewmember into good-enough health for safe containment in GenPop - in which they must be moved to GenPop with the **permission of the treating medical staff.** Medical should be made aware the crewmember is under arrest.
5. Leave the inmate with whoever is responsible for sentencing (by default, the Warden) after informing them of their crimes and communicating any notes about their behaviour during transport and arrest.

## Crimes
Crimes are split into six categories: Misdemeanours, Felonies, Grand Crimes, Extreme Crimes, Capital Crimes, and Crimes Against Sapience.

Each category has its own example of what a textbook case should aim for per crime:
- **Misdemeanour:** 1 minute
- **Felonies:** 4 minutes
- **Grand Crimes:** 5 minutes
- **Extreme Crimes:** 6 minutes
- **Capital Crimes:** 8 minutes
- **Crimes Against Sapience:** Permanent Confinement without Appeal

A list of all crimes can be found in the [dedicated Crimes page](/rules/internal-security-guidelines/crimes).
### Mutually Exclusive Crimes
Some crimes may not be charged together, either due to overlapping in their nature, or being the same crime at a separate severity. Such crimes include, grouped by their relations:
- **Theft** X-00 charges (Theft, Theft of Departmental Equipment, Theft of Weaponry, and Espionage)
- **Subversion of Justice** X-01, X-02, & X-03 Charges (Perjury, Obstruction of Justice, Forgery, Bribery, Espionage, Abuse of Power, Dereliction of Authority, Sedition)
- **Custody** X-03 & X-04 Charges (Breach of Custody, Breach of Confinement, Breach of Long-term Confinement, and Breach of Permanent Confinement).
- **Endangerment** X-05 Charges (Reckless Operation and Malpractice).
- **Trespassing** X-06 & X-07 charges (Trespassing, Breaking and Entering, Secure Trespass, Secure Breaking and Entering)
- **Bodily harm** X-08 & X-09 charges (Brawling, Assault, Manslaughter/Lethal Assault, Murder, Obstruction of Revival, Prevention of Revival, Mass Murder, Mass Prevention of Revival, Serial Murder)
- **Sabotage** X-10 & X-11 charges (Sabotage, Capital Sabotage, Irreparable Sabotage, and Broken Arrow).
- **Antisocial** X-14 to X-18 Charges (Fearmongering, Hooliganism, Vandalism, Brandishing, Vigilantism, Brawling, Kidnapping, and Black Marketeering)
## Sentence Caps
An inmate may only be subject to the maximum sentence of their highest tier crime. Any additions to their sentence post-brigging, such as attempts to escape, disorderly conduct within the GenPop cell, or failure to comply with the terms of their community service are not bound by the Sentence Cap.
- **Misdemeanour:** 5 minutes
- **Felonies:** 8 minutes
- **Grand Crimes:** 10 minutes
- **Extreme Crimes:** 12 minutes
- **Capital Crimes:** 15 minutes or Permanent Confinement with Appeal

In this case, the inmate would be charged with the following: *Breaking and Entering* (max sentence 8 minutes) and *Murder* (max sentence 10 minutes).

## Brig Time Modification

At the Sentencers discretion, certain modifiers may be applied to a sentence to increase or decrease the time served within the brig. These broadly fit into 4 categories.
You cannot stack multiple modifiers within the same type, nor are you obligated to use one from each category. These modifications can be made **before or after** a sentence is applied, depending on the circumstances. See the explainers below the table for more information.

| **Modifier**     | **Lesser**                                               | **Moderate**                                            | **Severe**                                      |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Intent**       | Accessory to a Charge<br>(at least -75% per crime) | Conspiracy<br>(at least -50% per crime)           | Attempted<br>(at least -25% per crime)    |
| **Additions**    | Evading Arrest<br>(up to +25% on total)                           | Violently Evading Arrest<br>(up to +50% on total)                | Targeting Central Command Staff<br>(+100% on total)      |
| **Deductions**   | Compliance<br>(up to -25%)                               | Confession<br>(Up to -50%)                              | Voluntary Tracking Implantation<br>(up to -80%  on total) |
| **Circumstance** | Good Behaviour<br>(up to -50% of remaining or parole)                 | Self-Defense or Performed under duress<br>(up to -100% per crime) | Delta Level Alert<br>(-100%, See Explainer)     |
### Intent
- **Accessory to a Charge:** Assistance with a crime. If someone provided access, tools, wilfully looked the other way, or otherwise facilitated the performance of a crime or helped cover for a crime, they can be charged for any and all crimes they assisted with, but only for up to a quarter the usual sentence time.
- **Conspiracy:** To conspire to commit a crime is to prepare and plan for said crime. Relevant proof must be presented to charge someone with Conspiracy to commit a crime. If the proof exists, they may be charged for each crime they can be Verified to have planned to commit, but only for up to half of the usual sentence time.
- **Attempted:** Attempting to commit a crime is just as much of a crime as actually committing a crime, however leeway can and should be given. Consider the severity of the crime, the degree of completion achieved, and the disposition of the arrested. For each crime attempted, they can be charged up to 75% of the usual sentence time.
### Additions
- **Evading Arrest:** Wilful evasion of a legal arrest can incur up to an additional 25% to the total sentence they are being arrested for.
- **Violently Evading Arrest:** Violently resisting or evading a legal arrest can incur up to an additional 50% to the total sentence they are being arrested for. The violence must be directed at the arresting Guard. The degree of violence should be considered when applying this modifier.
- **Targeting Central Command Staff:** The direct and wilful targeting of confirmed Central Command Staff, such as Admirals, Inspectors, and ERT, in a crime must Always incur an additional 100% time on the Total sentence.
### Deductions
- **Compliance:** Cordially complying with a legal arrest should result in a deduction of up to 25% on the total sentence. Cordially complying with a legal search that turns up stolen items should result in a deduction of up to 25% of the relevant theft crimes.
- **Confession:** Wilfully confessing to a crime and pleading guilty should result in a deduction of up to 50% of the relevant crimes. Sharing actionable intelligence, confessing accomplices, and revealing the location of hidden stolen items can result in a deduction of up to 50% of a total sentence or remaining sentence.
- **Voluntary Tracking Implantation:** An arrested person may volunteer for a tracking implant to receive up to an 80% deduction of their total sentence or remaining sentence. See Implantation Procedures.
### Circumstance
- **Good Behaviour:** An inmate who is behaving in a manner befitting that of a safe and functioning member of the crew may have their remaining sentence reduced by up to 50% at the discretion of the Warden, or may be a valid candidate for Parole.
- **Self-Defence or Performed Under Duress:** Any crime or crimes committed in self-defence, where the perpetrator reasonably believed they were in lethal danger if they did not commit it, and any crime performed Under Duress such as the threatening of themselves, a loved one, or their long-term material conditions, may have their sentences reduced by up to 100%. Any crime or crimes committed with the full belief that they were necessary in service to a reasonably believed lawful order may also have their sentences reduced by up to 100%.
- **Delta Level Alert:** During a Delta Level Alert, Security Personnel should be focusing on protecting the crew first and foremost, including ensuring the health and safety of inmates. Any crimes reasonably committed in the name of preventing the detonation of the Nuclear Device, or similar such dire emergencies, may have their sentences reduced by up to 100%.
## Sentence Types
The appropriate punishment for a crime or crimes is dependant on the total time of the sentence. The appropriate punishments listed in this document fall under three categories: 
- **Minor Sentences**: Sentences whose total times are less than or equal to 10 minutes
- **Major Sentences:** Sentences whose total times are greater than 10 minutes
- **Capital Sentences:** Sentences where a Capital Crime or greater was committed and the total time before the sentence cap is greater than 25 minutes.

Confinement of any type is considered the "default" appropriate punishment. Alternate appropriate punishments may be used to substitute part or all of time that would otherwise be spent in confinement.

Alternate punishments are to be offered and not forced. Any Head of Department may still force demotion, and if this is done in response to a crime it should come with a reduced sentence.
### Minor Sentences
#### Temporary Confinement
Also known as "light brigging". The inmate should only be lightly searched for items and objects that pose an active risk to other inmates or the structure of GenPop if there is a Reasonable Suspicion or prior knowledge of these items existing on their person.
The inmate should retain all access to job equipment, internals, clothing, PDAs, headsets, etc. while in Temporary Confinement.
#### Restriction of Departmental Equipment
Downgrade or suspend an inmates accesses or innate permits for equipment gained from their job. This should **only** be used if an inmate used said departmental equipment or access to commit crimes they could not have otherwise.
#### Community Service
Community Service may be offered by any member of the Security Department in place of an arrest. 

The Guidelines for a valid Community Service offer are as follows:
- Community Service may not involve active threats to the inmate's life or health.
- Community Service must not take an inmate off the station or outside of coordinate monitoring range.
- Community Service should end in 5 minutes, or in line with what they would have been sentenced with if they were jailed.
- Community Service can only be issued for Minor Sentences.
- An inmate **cannot be forced** to accept Community Service, and should always be offered the option of a Brig sentence instead.
- An inmate must be informed of the task assigned to them BEFORE accepting it.
- Failing to comply with accepted Community Service will instead result in an ***Obstruction of Justice charge***, and they should be detained for both their original sentence **and** the original charge.
- Failure to complete Community Service within the set time limit is **not** considered a failure if a legitimate attempt to complete it is observed.
- Community Service offers are a **one-time offer.** If one is rejected, another one cannot be offered, and an inmate cannot unrevoke their interest later into their sentence.
- An inmate performing Community Service should be marked in the security records system as on Parole
- The Head of Security may overrule a Security Guard’s Community Service charge, but not the Warden's.
### Major Sentences
#### Extended Confinement
A more extreme version of temporary confinement, where an inmate loses access to more privileges than for minor crimes. It is also known as "heavy brigging".
Inmates on Extended Confinement sentences should be dressed in prison uniforms and have their PDA access restricted. They may still be allowed clothes that do not interfere with their prisoner uniform.
#### Demotion
With the permission of the Head of Personnel, and either the permission or request of the inmates Head of Department, an inmate may be demoted to Passenger.
If the inmate is already a Passenger, their maintenance access should be removed.
Anyone who has been demoted by their Head of Department may only be rehired into a different department with their original Head's permission, and with approval from Central Command.
### Capital Sentences
Capital Sentences are the most extreme sentence a member of Security can give, and should be used sparingly. A Capital Sentence requires **explicit CO permission**. Capital Sentences can only be given for Capital Crimes, and may never be applied for anything below a Capital Crime - not even repeated offenders of lower crimes.
#### Permanent Confinement
Permanent Confinement, or “Perma”, is placing an inmate into the station brig for the rest of the shift until they can be processed at Central Command.
An inmate in Permanent Confinement may consent to any other form of Capital Sentencing **except** for Execution at any time during their sentence at Security's discretion.
Perma prisoners should be dressed in clear prisoner clothing, their PDA confiscated, and given a prisoner headset.
##### With Appeal
Any sentence of Permanent Confinement where the inmate **did not** commit a Crime Against Sapience or a Breach of Long-Term Confinement is considered to be ***Permanent Confinement With Appeal.***

Permanent Confinement With Appeal allows the inmate to have their **sentence reduced** to a Major Sentence or to **have it commuted** to Permanent Parole at the **discretion of the Warden** or Acting Warden.
This should be done for good behaviour and/or if the inmate no longer presents a danger to themself or the crew.
##### Without Appeal
Any sentence of Permanent Confinement where the inmate committed a Crime Against Sapience or a Breach of Long-Term Confinement is considered to be ***Permanent Confinement Without Appeal.***

Permanent Confinement Without Appeal **cannot be altered**, reduced, or commuted to Permanent Parole without the direct and verifiable intervention of Central Command.
#### Extradition
An inmate may be transported via secure transport to Central Command for investigation and handed over to SolGov.
During the transit time for the shuttle to arrive at the Station, the inmates' right to freedom of movement may be waived and an isolation cell used. If Central Command is unwilling or unable to arrange Extradition, another form of Capital Sentence **must** be used.
#### Stasis
An inmate may be placed into cryogenic storage with the intent to wake them up at Central Command for further processing.
A prisoner may choose to take a Stasis deal at any time by simply entering the cryopod within GenPop. Security may place any inmate on a permanent confinement sentence into Cryogenic storage should they remain SSD for longer than 10 minutes, or upon detection of them becoming Catatonic.
#### Execution
```
NOTE: all executions will be thoroughly reviewed by the Admin team. If you are going to execute someone, please make sure you have exhausted all avenues. “If your heart wavers, do not shoot.”
```
Executions are saved for the hardest or most unrepentant criminals. Inmates sentenced to execution are provided special rights before their execution, covered below:
- Right to a Trial before their peers, and Central Command (if available).
- Right to a last meal (provided upon request).
- Right to a “last call” (this can either be a letter delivered to a loved one, or a meeting with someone on station).
- Right to choose their form of execution within reason (see below).
These rights are **irrefutable and irrevocable**, even by the CO.

The Inmate sentenced to death may choose their form of execution from the following:
- **Firing Squad**
- **Lethal Injection** (If there is no Chemist, or Chemistry is not willing or able to provide a suitable method, an injection of Lexorin shall be provided to Security by Central Command)
- **Death by Inert Gas**
- **Lethal Spacewalk** (retrieval of the corpse is presumed, however an inmate may waive this right. Death must be confirmed in this case. The Cargo Shuttle or ATS may be used for this form of execution)
- **Servitorisation** (forced insertion into a NanoTrasen brand Cyborg Chassis or AI core).

> AN INMATE WITH A DO NOT BORG FORM, A DO NOT BORG MEDICAL RECORD, OR EXPLICITLY REVOKES THEIR CONSENT TO BE BORGED CAN NEVER BE SENTENCED TO EXECUTION VIA SERVITORISATION.

Inmates sentenced to execution may request alternate methods of execution that may be approved or denied at the discretion of the individual passing the sentence, as well as the CO.
If the inmate fails to choose in a reasonable time, or refuses, the sentencing security member is responsible for choosing the means of execution.

Any and all executions must be announced via the communications console explaining who was executed, why they were executed, and how.
## Inmate Rights
Individuals who are being housed in the brig, referred to henceforth as “inmates”, have immutable rights that NanoTrasen and any NanoTrasen staff must uphold.
- Inmates must be granted medical care for any injuries sustained during an arrest, and should not be brigged unless they are in good enough health that their condition will not deteriorate. Inmates may forfeit their right to medical care at their discretion. If they do so, that right remains forfeited until the end of their sentence.
- Inmates must be allowed access to their NanoTrasen-issued headsets, unless said radio access is abused to such an extent that jeopardizes their stay in the brig (inciting a riot, verbal abuse of NanoTrasen Security staff or members of the crew, threats of recidivism, etc.).
- Inmates must be granted clothing, food, water, shelter, safety, and a source of power (in the case of IPCs). If GenPop is compromised or rendered unsafe, confinement must be established in another safe location for the duration of the inmate's stay.
- Inmates must have access to their shift-mandated Personal Digital Assistant within the brig, unless they are in permanent confinement or evidence of tampering has been found.
- Inmates must be granted freedom of movement, and should not be restrained with handcuffs or other devices after incarceration in GenPop unless there is an undue risk to life or limb of themself, or security personnel. Blindfolds, gags, or straightjackets may not be used without direct authorization by the CMO, or the most senior medical staff on shift.
### Right to Reasonable Freedom
If the Brig is rendered insecure or breached by external forces and an inmate exits, they cannot be charged for this action.

Crimes that describe breaching or breaking out of the Brig can only be charged for **wilful and purposeful** escape attempts.

Inmates have the Right to Reasonable Freedom. If an Inmate believes their confinement area to be unsafe or unsecure and takes actions towards their self-preservation, they are within their rights to do so.
### Revival Rights

- If lethal force was required to incapacitate a capital offender, or a violent attempt to escape permanent confinement, they may have their revival delayed for a period of **up to 10 minutes** to allow Security to **recuperate and re-establish order** on the station. Inmates on a delayed-revival must be held in a body bag or on a stasis bed. If an inmate is allowed to rot during a delayed revival, Security may be held culpable for ***Prevention of Revival*** pending an investigation from Central Command.
- The CO of a NanoTrasen Station may overturn a Delayed Revival at their discretion if they believe it to be in the best interests of the crew, or if Security have failed to uphold their responsibility in using this right to delayed revival.
## Parole
A Warden, or the Acting Warden for the shift, may offer Parole to an inmate already in the brig so that they may complete the remainder of their sentence while interfacing with the rest of the station. The lawyer may petition Security for parole for an inmate who has consented to being represented by them, but may not authorize parole themself.

Any inmates on Parole should be escorted around the station by a Trusted Authority. Senior medical staff and the station Psychologist may also act as a Trusted Authority, with appropriate signed paperwork from the Head of Personnel, or the CO if the HoP is unavailable.

Security may only deny parole if they can prove there is demonstrable risk to the health or morale of the rest of the crew, and may not blanket-ban Parole - they must review each request on a case by case basis.

In the event of a red alert, Parolees must return to the brig for their safety effective immediately.

If an inmate abuses their privileges whilst on Parole, their sentence may be restarted, and an additional charge of ***Breach of Custody*** applied circumstantially.
## Pressing Charges & Withholding Charges
Any member of the crew, regardless of their rank or position, may press charges against any other crew member via raising this with an on-scene Security Guard, the Lawyer, their Head of Department, or any member of the Security department.
Only Security has the authority to act upon these charges in any way - all a Lawyer or Departmental Head is supposed to do is pass on the information gathered to Security.

An individual affected by a crime may not be charged if the victim of the crime explicitly drops the charges. This will pardon the accused of that charge.
This does not apply to crimes that affect more than one person, or the station as a whole (a chemist who had their plushie stolen may drop the theft charges, but if their chem master was bombed they may not drop the charges as this affects a whole department).

If a victim of a crime is rendered unable to comment on if they wish to press charges, the default assumption is that they consent to pressing charges for any and all crimes committed against them.
# Implantation
Sometimes, it is required to implant an inmate with a NanoTrasen-branded subdermal implant for the safety of themselves or others. It may also be required to remove third-party implants from within an inmate.

Any inmate that has been linked to a physical implanter may be subject to an implant removal at Security’s discretion. During the time in which it takes to remove an implant, an inmate's sentence may be **paused for up to 5 minutes**. After 5 minutes, their sentence timer should be **resumed**. If an implant is **not removed** in the duration of their sentence + 5 minutes, they should be **released without extraction** taking place.

Implant-related procedures, including implant removal, should ideally be performed with the assistance of the Medical Department and overview of the **CMO** or Acting CMO.
## Tracking Implants
Tracking implants are subdermal implants that forcefully activate a coordinate sensor suite that cannot be altered, much like a suit's sensor system. Security may use such an implant on an inmate for any violent Capital Crime, and **must** use such an implant on anyone who has committed a Crime Against Sapience.

An inmate may take a plea deal (as per the brig time modifiers table) to **willingly** take a tracking implant for a reduction on their sentence. This does not require them to be charged with a Capital Crime or greater, and **may be used for any tier** of charge.
Security is not obligated to fulfil this request, and should perform a cost/benefit analysis of losing an implanter, the time spent in prison, how much they would gain from submitting to a tracking implant, and if it is even required. Security should consider every request on a case-by-case basis.
## Mindshields
The standard-issue patented SolGov beta-wave mental shielding chip informally known as the Mindshield is an implant that disrupts and prevents foreign influences from manipulating the brain. Mindshields should be used when an inmate is suspected of being under the influence of malicious forces not explained by blackmail or coercion.

Mindshields may be used in two ways by NanoTrasen Security Guards: Mind-flashing and Permanent Shielding.
### Mind-Flashing
A “mind-flash” operation is the act of implanting a Mindshield long enough to break the control over an inmate, **then removing it** the moment control is broken.

This should be performed if breaking mental control is required, but the inmate cannot or will not consent to permanent mental shielding. The inmate should be made verbally aware that it is temporary. A NanoTrasen Implant Remover is required to be had on-hand for this process.
### Permanent Shielding
An inmate may consent to permanent mental shielding if requested by Security. In this case, they will sign a form detailing their agreement to permanent shielding and be implanted with a Mindshield. After this point, any removal of the Mindshield should result in a charge of ***Unlawful Removal of a Permanent Mindshield.***
Once the form is signed, Security may waive all charges the inmate was accused of during the period of unlawful control.
# Crime Scene Investigation
In the event of a confirmed crime or other incident requiring the attention of the Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator, the area where the incident occurred is defined as a Crime Scene.

The Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator has automatic authority and right to enter any Crime Scene actively under investigation, and does not require any Warrant, Permit, or other form of paperwork to do so. The Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator may extend this authority to one other member of the Security Department, where relevant.
Only the CO may cancel this authority, unless the CO is themself under relevant suspicion.

During a Crime Scene Investigation, the Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator should aim to answer the following questions:
- **What Crime** or other relevant incident has occurred?
- **Who** is Responsible?
- What was their **Intent**?
- What **Evidence** can be obtained to support any conclusions?

The goal of a Crime Scene Investigation is to obtain evidence capable of **proving** Reasonable Suspicion, Lethal Intent, Conspiracy to Commit, or any other ISG related item that requires proof, evidence, or verification.

The Detective or Acting Crime Scene Investigator should aim to disrupt the standard activities of the employees of the station as little as possible, be as unobtrusive as possible, and should aim to finish the investigation as quickly as possible so that the employees can resume their standard work and generate profit.
