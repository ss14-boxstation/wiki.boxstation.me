# Mutually Exclusive Crimes
Some crimes may not be charged together, either due to overlapping in their nature, or being the same crime at a separate severity. Such crimes include, grouped by their relations:
- **Theft** X-00 charges (Theft, Theft of Departmental Equipment, Theft of Weaponry, and Espionage)
- **Subversion of Justice** X-01, X-02, & X-03 Charges (Perjury, Obstruction of Justice, Forgery, Bribery, Espionage, Abuse of Power, Dereliction of Authority, Sedition)
- **Custody** X-03 & X-04 Charges (Breach of Custody, Breach of Confinement, Breach of Long-term Confinement, and Breach of Permanent Confinement).
- **Endangerment** X-05 Charges (Reckless Operation and Malpractice).
- **Trespassing** X-06 & X-07 charges (Trespassing, Breaking and Entering, Secure Trespass, Secure Breaking and Entering)
- **Bodily harm** X-08 & X-09 charges (Brawling, Assault, Manslaughter/Lethal Assault, Murder, Obstruction of Revival, Prevention of Revival, Mass Murder, Mass Prevention of Revival, Serial Murder)
- **Sabotage** X-10 & X-11 charges (Sabotage, Capital Sabotage, Irreparable Sabotage, and Broken Arrow).
- **Antisocial** X-14 to X-18 Charges (Fearmongering, Hooliganism, Vandalism, Brandishing, Vigilantism, Brawling, Kidnapping, and Black Marketeering)
# Misdemeanours
Sentences where the highest tier crime is a Misdemeanour **cannot exceed** a total brig time of **5 minutes.**

For a first offence or minor offence Misdemeanour, **a warning** should be given first.
For repeat offence or multiple offence Misdemeanour, **up to 1 minute** can be given per crime.
- **1-00 Theft**
    To take personal property without consent.
- **1-03 Failure to Comply**
    To wilfully ignore lawful orders given by a superior in the workplace, or an order related to the Security Guidelines by a member of Security.
- **1-05 Reckless Operation**
    To operate machinery, vessels, equipment, or weapons in a way that could recklessly cause harm to themselves or an individual, or under the influence of decision impairing substances.
- **1-06 Trespassing**
    To enter an area and refuse to leave where one is unauthorized and/or uninvited. Trespass is simply the act of entering an area uninvited.
    If other crimes are committed, they should be charged with breaking and entering instead.
- **1-08 Brawling**
    To bodily harm another crew member without consent and without critting or killing. Can only be charged if the victim presses charges.
- **1-13 Animal Cruelty**
    To inflict unnecessary suffering on a non-invasive, non-culinary animal with malicious intent. Applies to all station pets, or any normally invasive or culinary animals that are clearly labelled as a pet.
- **1-14 Hooliganism**
    To intentionally engage in disruptive conduct. Includes but is no way limited to harassment, slander, abuse of radio/intercoms, and other disruptive behaviours that promote a poor working environment.
- **1-15 Vandalism**
    To deface or superficially damage public property, or property belonging to an individual.
- **1-16 Fearmongering**
    To deliberately cause fear or alarm through falsehood, rumour, or misinformation.
- **1-17 Brandishing**
    To wield a firearm or other deadly weapon in public without explicit permission or valid reason of self defence or defence of others.
- **1-18 Vigilantism**
    To take it upon yourself to enforce the ISG outside emergency circumstances, or without authorization from the CO or HoS.
# Felonies
Sentences where the highest tier crime is a Felony **cannot exceed** a total brig time of **8 minutes**.

For a Felony, a textbook case should aim for **4 minutes per crime**.
- **2-00 Theft of Departmental Equipment**
    The theft of equipment specific to one department such as defibs, chemicals, mining equipment, or RCDs. Does not necessarily cover things WITHIN a department such as materials. Not all equipment in a department is departmental equipment. Use the best judgement.
- **2-01 Forgery**
    To falsify paperwork, either through modification or impersonation of a Trusted Authority
- **2-02 Perjury**
    To wilfully and maliciously lie to Security in matters of the ISG
- **2-03 Obstruction of Justice**
    To wilfully obstruct, interfere with, or prevent a lawful arrest on another
	To wilfully ignore a valid warrant
	To breach terms of Community Service
- **2-05 Malpractice**
    To perform your job poorly or negligently in a way that endangers the safety of the crew. Covers Scientific, Engineering, and Medical malpractice as one charge.
- **2-06 Secure Trespass**
    To trespass into an area marked as Secure or Confidential within [SOP](/rules/standard-operating-procedures).
- **2-07 Breaking and Entering**
    To trespass with the specific intent of committing a crime, or committing a crime during trespass.
	To trespass through means of damaging the station.
- **2-08 Manslaughter**
  To be charged when through action or inaction someone's life signs cease without lethal intent.
- **2-09 Assault**
    To cause grievous physical harm, or to force unwanted physical contact on an individual or individuals. Or to threaten such actions with suspected capability and intent to do so or follow through.
- **2-10 Sabotage**
    The disruption of regular station operations to a degree that inconveniences or endangers other members of staff or the profitability of the station.
- **2-12 Malicious use of Substances**
    To force upon another crew member harmful, impeding, or mind altering substances.
    Overdosing medicine or other forms of medical-related harm should be charged with Malpractice instead.
- **2-14 Recidivism**
    To intentionally reoffend with the same charge or greater on multiple (3+) occasions after already being charged for that crime.
- **2-16 Kidnapping**
    To unlawfully restrain an individual or to forcefully relocate and individual without their consent.
- **2-18 Black Marketeering**
    The production and/or supplying of Contraband without a permit.
    The illicit use or funding of NanoTrasen ordering consoles.
# Grand Crimes
Sentences where the highest tier crime is a Grand Crime **cannot exceed** a total brig time of **10 minutes.**

For a Grand Crime, a textbook case should aim for **5 minutes per crime**.
- **3-00 Theft of Weaponry**
		To through force or deception acquire firearms or lethal weaponary from the lawful owner of a weapon, such as a security officer, command member, or authorized armed crew members (salvage specialists, bartenders etc).
- **3-02 Bribery**
    To willingly accept material or financial possessions in exchange for normally illegal restricted items or services.
    The person who bribed should be charged for the relevant crimes they commit via bribery (*Espionage* for command items, *Obstruction of Justice* for silencing people, *Forgery* for expediting paperwork etc).
- **3-03 Breach of Custody**
    To escape custody or breach terms of parole
- **3-06 Secure Breaking and Entering**
    To trespass into an area marked as Secure or Confidential within [SOP](/rules/standard-operating-procedures) with the specific intent of committing a crime, or committing a crime during trespass. To trespass into an area marked as Secure or Confidential within SOP through means of damaging the station.
- **3-09 Murder**
    To cause an individuals life signs to cease with lethal intent.
    The victim must not have perished due to lack of medical attention. There ***must*** be proof of Lethal Intent.
    - This proof can be obtained in multiple ways, including  previous track of Death Threats, a Confession of lethal intent, severe injuries indicating the victim did not merely bleed out or suffocate after falling unconscious, or the usage of excessively powerful weaponry  (Such as a C20r on an unarmoured target).
    - Security should aim to prove lethal intent via talking with the suspect primarily, and should only rely on context clues if they are clear, or if the situation itself is unclear.
- **3-12 Malicious use of Stimulants**
    To use stimulants or substances to reduce the effectiveness of non-lethal methods, or to use stimulants or substances to prolong an unlawful firefight.
# Extreme Crimes
Sentences where the highest tier crime is an Extreme Crime **cannot exceed** a total brig time of **12 minutes.**

For an Extreme Crime, a textbook case should aim for **6 minutes per crime**.
- **4-00 Espionage**
    To steal Command Staff gear, the Captains Gear, and the gear of Central Command Officials.
    To disguise as trusted NanoTrasen personnel.
- **4-01 Abuse of Power**
		To misuse authority granted by Central Command at the start of shift as a member of Command or Security, or to misue authority granted through in-situ promotion for unlawful means.
- **4-02 Dereliction of Authority**
		To knowingly abandon ones post within a position of authority, or leave duties nessicary to station function unattended.
- **4-03 Breach of Confinement**
    To purposefully escape legal Confinement, such as any cell, brig, or GenPop one has been placed in within the bounds of the ISG.
- **4-05 Unlawful Removal of a Permanent Mindshield**
		To remove a lawfully implanted mindshield of a mindshielded individual without proper procedure.
- **4-08 Obstruction of Revival**
    To cause an individual to be greatly more difficult to revive, while still remaining somewhat reasonably possible to revive, such as by:
    - hiding a body from medical personnel
	- discarding a body into NT Orbital Space
	- poisoning or irradiating a body past standard revival thresholds
	- damaging a body beyond reasonable medical capabilities
	- actively and wilfully accelerating decomposition.
# Capital Crimes
Sentences where the highest tier crime is a Capital Crime **cannot exceed** a total brig time of **15 minutes.**
If the sentence reaches a time **greater than 25 minutes** before the sentence cap is applied, and/or the severity of the crimes committed warrant it, a sentence of **Permanent Confinement With Appeal may be applied instead.** 

For a Capital Crime, a textbook case should aim for **8 minutes per crime**.
- **5-01 Sedition**
    To overthrow a lawfully established Chain of Command without legitimate cause.
	To ignore a lawful and legitimate Vote of No Confidence.
- **5-08 Mass Murder**
    To crit and/or kill multiple (3+) people in succession.
    (Death or Critting of security personnel in self-defence cannot be considered for Mass Murder)
- **5-09 Prevention of Revival**
    To prevent a body from being revived by the medical department, such as by:
    - discarding a body beyond NT Orbital Space
    - rendering a body unrecognizable through force, heat, or decomposition
    - forcibly cloning an individual without their consent
- **5-10 Capital Sabotage**
    The theft or destruction of irreplaceable Central Command gear, such as the Thermal Exchange Generator (TEG),Station Anchor, Gravity Generator or the Nuclear Device.
# Crimes Against Sapience
Any Crime Against Sapience **must carry** with it the sentence of **Permanent Confinement Without Appeal.**
If **three or more** Crimes Against Sapience were committed, and no Brig Modifier Deductions or Circumstance apply, **a Trial for Execution may be warranted**.

Accessory to, Conspiracy to commit, or an Attempt of a Crime Against Sapience should be charged as a Capital Crime.
- **6-03 Breach of Long-term Confinement**
    To purposefully breach Permanent Confinement with Appeal
- **6-04 Breach of Permanent Confinement**
    To purposefully escape Permanent Confinement without Appeal.
    The inmate has demonstrated a lack of ability to be contained, so this crime is grounds for legal execution.
- **6-05 Non-consensual Servitorization**
    To place an individual inside any law-bound silicon, such as a Cyborg or AI Core, without their explicit consent.
- **6-08 Mass Prevention/Obstruction of Revival**
	To render multiple (3+) individuals beyond the reasonable capacity of revival.
- **6-09 Serial Murder**
    To kill multiple (3+) individuals separately with clear repeated patterns in how they were killed.
- **6-10 Irreparable Sabotage**
    Damaging the structural integrity and/or habitability of the station beyond reasonable repair, such as a Tesloose, Singuloose, removal of vital gases, breaching of key atmospheric miners, making the temperature inhospitable, mass spacing of common areas, etc.
- **6-11 Broken Arrow**
    To activate or detonate a Nuclear Device without approval from Central Command.
- **6-12 Bioterrorism**
		To purposefully introduce a deadly viral agent or other bioweapons (such as Romerol) onto the station with the purpose of indiscriminate infection or harm of the crew.
- **6-13 Cannibalism**
    To consume any part of any sapient being, living or dead.
